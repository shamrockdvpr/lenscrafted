Getting Started

Your first camera

You just got your first camera, take that lens cap off and power it on and check out these settings

Shutter Speed
Usually a dial on top, displayed on your screen as a fraction (1/500, 1/250 etc). This controls how long the shutter is open and allows you to control how much light gets to the camera. See the lighting tutorial for more details on light.
Aperature
This controls the size of the opening that lets in light. The larger the aperature the more light is let in. This is shown as a f number (f22, f6, f15 etc) and the larger the number the smaller the opening.
ISO
This is the camera's sensitivity to light. A very high ISO can lead to grainy photos. Most cameras are set around 400 ISO
White Balance
This controls how the photo changes colors by adjusting the color that is read as white.
Zoom/Lens Angle
This is usually controlled by twisiting the center of the lens. This allows you to zoom in for a tighter shot or zoom out for a wide angle shot.
Focus
This is usually controlled by twisting the end of the lens, this controls how the camera focuses on the subject. By pairing focus with various settings you can achieve interesting photos such as depth of field.
