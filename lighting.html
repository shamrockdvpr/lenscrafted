Lighting

Exposure

The main aspect to a good photo is proper exposure. Exposure is controlled using the three main settings, ISO, Shutter Speed and Aperature. If a photo is underexposed, increasing shutter speed may work, or increasing ISO, or creating a larger aperature. However each setting has drawbacks.

Shutter Speed
There are a few drawbacks to increasing or decreasing shutter speed. A high shutter speed may not let enough light in and can make motion apear frozen, which may not be the look you are going for. Conversly a low shutter speed needs a tripod or stable shooting surface to prevent excessive blur.
Aperature
Aperature also creates a depth of field effect. A low aperature may let less light in but also creates an infinite depth of field, which can be detrimental to the photo layout. On the other hand, a high aperature creates a shallow depth of field which may not be the look you are going for.
ISO
A higher or lower ISO allows more freedom with the other settings but using a high ISO can result in very grainy photos. And using a low ISO can make a photo much too underexposed or require very high settings from the other two to make it work.

These three settings form what is known as the Exposure Triangle. To properly expose a photo, a good balance of all three is required.
